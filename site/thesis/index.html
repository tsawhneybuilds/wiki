Thesis walkthrough

Credit can build factories, or merely inflate balance sheets.

The thesis asks whether production-oriented finance helps regions build the supplier networks needed for durable manufacturing growth, and whether switching to foreign sourcing can leave domestic supplier capacity harder to rebuild. The current work is a research design plus an auditable India manufacturing pipeline. It is not yet a finished empirical result.

What Work Was Done

1. Thesis design

Framed the supplier-network question around production credit, non-production credit, domestic processed inputs, foreign sourcing, and manufacturing hysteresis.

2. Measurement plan

Specified detailed processed-input breadth, supplier-demand exposure, production-bank exposure, and foreign-sourcing exposure.

3. India evidence machine

Built an ASI-focused pipeline for selection, scale-up, catch-up, productivity allocation, and formal-informal reallocation.

4. Data audit

Inventoried real ASI source blocks and identified the gates: state matching, identifier stability, deflators, weights, and missing-value rules.

5. Dashboard prototype

Made a public-facing dashboard structure with a persistent warning that all displayed numbers are synthetic placeholders.

6. Replication checks

Added tests for schema validation, decomposition identities, panel-linkage gating, reproducible synthetic data, and safe dashboard exports.

Hypotheses, Tests, Findings

Hypothesis How to test it Current read
Production-oriented local banks help supplier ecosystems grow when demand rises. Interact county supplier-demand exposure with local production-bank exposure from deposits and C&I lending shares. Open Designed, not yet estimated on validated real data.
Non-production credit is weaker for manufacturing capacity. Compare production-bank exposure with real-estate, consumer, securities, and nonbank-finance credit exposure. Open Not yet proved or disproved.
Supplier-network dependence matters. Measure detailed processed-input breadth from input-output tables and test whether demand shocks matter more in network-dependent industries. Open Measurement plan exists; causal evidence is pending.
Foreign sourcing can erode domestic supplier capacity. Build foreign-sourcing exposure and test linked upstream establishments, employment, payroll, births, deaths, and diversity. Open No confirmed erosion finding yet.
Lost supplier capacity creates hysteresis. Test whether recovery is slower where the old supplier base deteriorated, and whether production credit weakens that persistence. Open This remains the central thesis test.
Indian manufacturing gaps reflect selection, scale-up, catch-up, and formal-informal reallocation. Use ASI plant data, productivity decomposition, state profiles, and eventually NSS/ASUSE and benchmark comparisons. Blocked The pipeline exists, but state-level claims are not release-ready.

What Was Found

Positive finding

The ASI source base is large enough to make a plant-year panel plausible. Repeated IDs are common enough to justify linkage work, and the code can produce auditable synthetic dashboard outputs.

Negative finding

The existing evidence is not yet strong enough to classify states, estimate entry and exit, claim productivity effects, or say that foreign sourcing eroded supplier capacity.

The most important result is discipline: the project currently supports a thesis-worthy research question and a reproducible pipeline, not a polished empirical conclusion.
